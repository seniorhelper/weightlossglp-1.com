GLP-1 & Cardiovascular Risk Reduction (SELECT Trial)

NEJM SELECT Trial 2023 · Semaglutide 2.4mg · N=17,604 · 3.3 yr follow-up

Bottom line: The SELECT trial — the largest cardiovascular outcomes trial in obesity medicine history — found that semaglutide reduced major cardiovascular events by 20% in adults with overweight or obesity and established cardiovascular disease. This was a 17,604-person, 3.3-year study published in the New England Journal of Medicine (2023).

Heart Disease Risk

People with obesity have roughly twice the risk of coronary heart disease. The SELECT trial demonstrated that GLP-1 therapy may directly address this — reducing cardiovascular events by 20%.

CDC Heart Disease & Obesity, NEJM SELECT 2023

Stroke Risk

Obesity is associated with a roughly 50% increased risk of ischemic stroke. Blood pressure, insulin resistance, and vascular inflammation are all contributing factors that GLP-1 therapy may help address.

American Heart Association, 2021

Cancer Types Linked to Obesity

The CDC and NIH have identified 13 types of cancer associated with overweight and obesity — including breast, colon, endometrial, kidney, and pancreatic cancer. Excess body fat is a documented carcinogen driver.

CDC Overweight & Obesity, NIH NCI 2022

Sleep Apnea Risk

Obesity is the single largest risk factor for obstructive sleep apnea. Neck fat, airway tissue, and breathing mechanics are all affected. Weight loss via GLP-1 therapy may significantly reduce apnea severity.

Sleep Foundation / AASM Clinical Guidelines

Knee Osteoarthritis Risk

Each pound of excess body weight adds approximately 4 pounds of pressure on the knee joints. Obesity is one of the strongest modifiable risk factors for osteoarthritis and joint replacement surgery.
